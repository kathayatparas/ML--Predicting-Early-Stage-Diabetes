Dataset Details¶

The dataset has been collected by M M Faniqul Islam, Rahatara Ferdousi, Yasmin Bushra, Sadikur Rahman and Humayrausing through direct questionnaires from the patients of Sylhet Diabetes Hospital in Sylhet, Bangladesh and approved by a doctor.

This dataset contains the sign and symptom data of newly diabetic or would be diabetic patient. Following is the list of attributes present in the dataset:

  1. Age - Range (20-65) years - Type : Integer
  2. Gender - 1. Male, 2.Female - Type : Object
  3. Polyuria - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  4. Polydipsia - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  5. sudden weight loss - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  6. weakness - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  7. Polyphagia - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  8. Genital thrush - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  9. visual blurring - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  10. Itching - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  11. Irritability - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  12. delayed healing - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  13. partial paresis - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  14. muscle stiffness - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  15. Alopecia - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  16. Obesity - 1.Yes, 2.No. - Type : Object
  17. class - 1.Positive, 2.Negative.

Dataset features to be included in the project are as follows:

Name Data Type Units Brief Description

Irritability Object Unknown Patients experienced tendency to be easily annoyed.
delayed healing Object Unknown Patients experienced delayed healing of wounds.
partial paresis Object Unknown Condition involves weakening of muscle or muscle group.
muscle stiffness Object Unknown Patients experienced muscle soreness in their body.
Alopecia Object Unknown Partial or complete absence of hair from where it normally grows.
Obesity Object Unknown Condition of being extremely fat or overweight.
class Object Unknown Result if the patient is diabetic or not.

Target Feature¶

The response(target) feature will be class attribute. The class attribute represents the result of diabetic test(positive or negative). We will build a predictive model that would predict based on the selected features if a person would be diabetic or not.

Goals and Objectives¶

Diabetes is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, is too high. Over time, having too much glucose in your blood can cause health problems, such as heart disease, nerve damage, eye problems, and kidney disease. You can take steps to prevent diabetes or manage it.[1]

About 422 million people worldwide have diabetes, the majority living in low-and middle-income countries, and 1.6 million deaths are directly attributed to diabetes each year. Both the number of cases and the prevalence of diabetes have been steadily increasing over the past few decades.[2]

The objective here is to identify features that are most significant in detecting early stages of diabetes. Diabetes symptoms are so mild that they are hard to spot it at first.[3] This analysis will not only explore the significance of the factors but also predict if a person is classified as a diabetic or not. This analysis will be able to spread awareness among people so that early detection and relevant actions could be taken so as to reduce further damage of diabetes on the body.

Summary & Conclusions¶

After data exploration, we can observe that a most of the patients, around 62.18% are male and only 37.82% are female. Around 60.68% of patients have been diagnosed with diabetes.

Looking at the bar charts we can observe that a significant number of female patients have been diagnosed positive for diabetes whereas a relatively less number of male patients have been diagnosed positive. Moreover, there are more male patients who have diagnosed negative for diabetes and that clearly represents that female patients are susceptible to diabetes.

It is also observed that a majority of patients diagnosed positive with diabetes also poses medical conditions like Polyuria, Polydipsia, Partial Paresis and Irritablility. However, a large number of patients that were diagnosed positive did not poses conditions like Polyphagia, Partial Paresis, Alopecia and Irritability.

After categorizing patients based on their gender and age group, we observed that female from age group 30-49 years are highly susceptible to diabetes followed by those female patients who are in their late 50-65 years. However, for male patients, no such pattern detected for diabetic condition as a large number of male patients have been diagnosed negative for the age group 20-29 and 30-49 years.

Clearly, Obesity and Genital Thrush does not play a major role in determining patient's diabetic condition. Similarly, sudden weight loss can not significantly determine patient's diabetic condition. However, female patients diagnosed positive with diabetes also experience a sudden loss in their weight. Also, patients diagnosed positive with diabetes tend to experience weakness in their body and a slightly vision blur in female patients.

Among the patients diagnosed positive, female patients have a higher tendency of having Polyphagia as compared to male patients.
